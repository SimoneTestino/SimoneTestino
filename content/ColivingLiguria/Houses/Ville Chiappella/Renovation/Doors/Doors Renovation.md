> [!Abstract]
> This document outlines a plan for a major internal renovation project: the in-house design and manufacturing of all new doors for [[Ville Chiappella]]. This is envisioned as a long-term, low-risk, and high-impact project suitable for volunteers and residents. The core idea is to combine simple woodwork (for the door slab) with 3D-printed components (for handles and latch mechanisms) to dramatically reduce costs and create custom, high-quality fittings.

### 1. Why Focus on Doors?

Doors are a critical component of any home, and in our project, they serve several functions beyond just closing a room:

* **Tactile Experience:** The handle of a door is one of the most frequently touched objects in a house. We want this to be a high-quality, pleasant experience.
* **Thermal Insulation:** Well-fitted doors with proper gaskets (seals) are essential for maintaining room temperature and saving on heating costs.
* **Sound Insulation:** To make shared living comfortable for all residents (especially remote workers), good acoustic insulation between rooms is non-negotiable.
* **General Safety:** Doors provide privacy and a sense of security. While our overall approach to security is community-based (see **[[Safety]]**), functional, lockable doors are a basic need.

### 2. The Problem & The Opportunity

**The Problem:** The existing doors in the houses are either missing, broken, or too old to be functional. They all need to be thrown away and replaced.

**The Opportunity:** Buying new, high-quality doors for an entire complex is extremely expensive. However, manufacturing doors is a feasible in-house project.
* It is a **low-risk** activity (primarily woodworking and 3D printing).
* It is **time-intensive**, making it a perfect long-term project for dedicated volunteers.
* It is a **fun, creative process** that combines digital design with hands-on manufacturing.

### 3. Project Scope: Internal vs. External Doors

This plan makes a clear distinction between two types of doors:

1.  **External Doors:** These have high-security and weather-proofing requirements. This project *does not* cover external doors for now, which will be part of a more elaborate, security-focused plan.
2.  **Internal Doors:** These are the primary focus of this project. The goal is to replace every single door between rooms, bathrooms, and corridors.

### 4. The Manufacturing Plan (Internal Doors)

The core of the project is to break the door down into manageable components, manufacturing each one ourselves.

* **1. The Digital Design (Fusion 360):**
    * A detailed, parameter-based 3D model of a complete door system (slab, frame, handle, latch) will be designed in **Fusion 360**.
    * This digital model will serve as the master blueprint for all parts.

* **2. The Door Slab (Woodworking):**
    * To keep costs minimal and manufacturing simple, the main body of the door (the slab) will be constructed from a **single, complete layer of quality wood**.
    * This will be a straightforward woodworking task: cutting the slab to size, routing edges, and creating pockets for the 3D-printed latch mechanism.

* **3. The Handle & Latch (3D Printing):**
    * The entire mechanism‚Äîthe handle, the internal latch, the lock (if any), and the faceplates‚Äîwill be **3D printed**.
    * This allows for full customisation, rapid production, and near-zero cost compared to buying dozens of metal locksets.

* **4. The Frame & Gasket (Finishing):**
    * We will also manufacture the door frames (the jambs that attach to the wall) ourselves.
    * This allows us to create a perfect fit and, most importantly, integrate a **rubber gasket (seal)** around the entire frame, ensuring excellent sound and thermal insulation.

### 5. Team & Collaboration

This is a multi-disciplinary project that requires close collaboration between several of our core teams:

* **[[Software Team|üë®‚Äçüíª Software Team]]**: Will be responsible for the technical 3D modeling, creating and managing the parametric **Fusion 360** project files.
* **[[Architecture Team|üèõÔ∏è design team]]**: The designers within our architecture team will lead the aesthetic and material choices. This includes selecting the wood, the paint colours, and designing the ergonomic and visual shape of the 3D-printed handles.
* **[[Construction Team|üõ†Ô∏è Construction Team]]**: Will oversee the hands-on physical manufacturing, from woodworking to assembly, and provide practical feedback on the design's "manufacturability" and durability.

### 6. Project Goals & Benefits

By taking this in-house approach, we aim to:

1.  **Drastically Reduce Costs:** Avoid the massive expense of purchasing 20-30+ new, pre-made doors.
2.  **Ensure High Quality:** If we do it well, we can produce a final product that is of higher quality (especially in terms of sound/thermal insulation) than a cheap commercial door.
3.  **Create a Scalable Model:** This project will serve as a template for a future, more complex project for manufacturing our own **external doors**.
