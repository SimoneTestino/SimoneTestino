### What We Offer

**1. Accommodation & Living Conditions:**

- **Rooms**: We offer beds in either **private or shared rooms**. When you apply, please state your room preference.

- **Initial Conditions**: Be prepared for rustic living. The houses are in various states of repair. Your room **may not have electricity** at first, and the bathroom might be shared and accessible via **exterior stairs**.

**2. Beds, Linens, and Cleanliness:**

- **Linens & Beds**: We provide **bed sheets, towels**, and either an existing bed or a new, high-quality air mattress. It is **appreciated if you can bring your own** linens.

- **Cleanliness**: Your room will be **deep-cleaned** before arrival. "Clean" means hygienic, not necessarily renovated.

**3. Food & Community Living:**

- **Shared Food System**: Food is **not included**. We operate a communal system based on a **shared weekly contribution** for groceries. We are a **vegan-friendly space**.

- **Volunteer-Led Activities**: Residents are encouraged to **propose and organize activities for the group**, such as hikes, beach trips, or city visits.

**4. Pet Policy:**

- **Pets are welcome!** There is large outdoor space, and pets have designated indoor areas to sleep. Most people incoming will bring their pet and we expect to have as many people as pets staying in the place! Keep in mind though that they are **not allowed in active renovation zones**.

**5. Amenities:**

- **Internet**: A connection **will be available from day one** via a personal mobile hotspot (approx. 15-30 Mbps). A faster 200Mbps connection is planned by the half of November; check the [[Calendar]] for updates.

- **Electricity & Heating**: There will be a **communal charging station**. Initial heating will be from a **wood stove**.

**6. Personal and Professional Growth:**

- **Launch Your Own Project**: Beyond the daily tasks, you can [[Propose a Project|propose]] and develop your own initiatives. We host a range of resident-led [[Supplementary Projects]] and encourage you to bring your ideas to the table.

- **Startup Ecosystem**: Coliving Liguria is designed as an environment to foster new ventures. If you have entrepreneurial ambitions, you can learn more about how we support [[ColivingLiguria for Startups and Cofounders]].

### What We Expect

**1. The Mission:**

Your contribution is to help transform the houses into beautiful, functional spaces by taking care of them and participating in their improvement. The first tasks will be **deep cleaning** followed by **light renovation**.

**2. Main Task Areas:**

- **Deep Cleaning & Light Renovation**: This involves cleaning spaces untouched for decades and clearing out unwanted items.

- **Community Tasks**: Active participation in daily life, such as cooking, cleaning, grocery runs, and tending the wood stove.

- **Gardening & DIY Garden Construction**: While our first priority is making the houses comfortable, we welcome ideas and projects for managing and improving the gardens from the very beginning.

**3. Contribution Level:**

- **Full-Time Volunteers**: The schedule is flexible. You should expect to contribute **25-30 hours per week** towards the care and improvement of the property.
- **Part-Time Volunteers**: We also offer a part-time option for those who wish to contribute fewer hours. This option includes a small rental fee of **under ‚Ç¨200 per person**. **Priority will be given to applicants interested in this option.**

### The Legal Framework & Requirements

**Your Role as a "Comodatario"**

It is essential to understand the legal nature of your stay. While we use the term "volunteer" colloquially, legally you will be a **`comodatario`** (a resident under a free rental contract) governed by a formal **`comodato d'uso`** agreement. Your sole obligation is to take care of the property. For a complete explanation, please read the [[Limiti Legali del Volontariato]] page.

- **Financial Independence (Required)**: The `comodato` contract can be terminated at any time. Therefore, it is **mandatory that you can prove sufficient financial means to be fully independent** in case the agreement ends unexpectedly.

- **Utilities**: For the first 1-2 months, utilities are included. Afterward, a contribution of up to **‚Ç¨50 per month** may be required for full-time volunteers, and if so, the expected weekly hours of house care will be **proportionally reduced**. Part-time volunteers will have utilities included in their rental fee.

- **Administrative Requirements**: To formalise the agreement, you must:

    - Have a **valid visa** for Italy, if applicable.

    - Provide a digital copy of a **valid, officially recognised ID** (ID card or passport).

    - Have your own **health insurance** valid in Italy and be prepared to manage any medical issues independently from ColivingLiguria.
