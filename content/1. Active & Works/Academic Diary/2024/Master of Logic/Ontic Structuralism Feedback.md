I just received a **6** on the Dutch grading scale for my work on [[Ontic Structuralism of Concepts]], a paper I had even considered submitting to the mentioned conference. I then received detailed feedback from Giorgio on how to improve the essay (28.01.2025), despite the mark already being final, in case I still wanted to refine the project and submit it to the conference.

First of all, I remain dissatisfied with the feedback because it focuses solely on structure. I cannot see form as the most important aspect of a paper; this is not what I value in my own essays, nor what I believe others should value in any non-didactic context. Moreover, structuring a paper is something AI will soon be able to handle for us. I have already expressed these frustrations in [[What I don't Enjoy in Philosophical Writing]], so I will now focus on what to do next.

I wanted to submit the paper to the conference primarily to strengthen my [[PhD Applications]], especially given my reflections in [[PhD Applications Regrets]].
