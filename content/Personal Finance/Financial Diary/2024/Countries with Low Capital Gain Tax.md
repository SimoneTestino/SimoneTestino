Given my [[Personal Finance]] this is an information that I take into consideration when deciding where to move for the longer run. This table has been made by ChatGPT 4.5 Deep Search, the Netherlands should be added (see [[Taxes in the Netherlands]]).

| Country            | CGT Rate        | Conditions and Exemptions                                                                                                                                     |
| ------------------ | --------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Belgium**        | 0% *(soon 10%)* | No CGT on shares under normal private management. A new 10% CGT with allowance is planned (2024).[^1][^2]                                                     |
| **Czech Republic** | 0%              | No CGT if shares are held for at least 3 years. Annual exemption up to CZK 100,000 if holding period is shorter.[^3]                                          |
| **Greece**         | 0%              | No CGT for private investors holding less than 0.5% of a listed company.[^3]                                                                                  |
| **Luxembourg**     | 0%              | No CGT on long-term private holdings. Additional exemption: €50,000 every 10 years.[^3][^4]                                                                   |
| **Slovakia**       | 0%              | No CGT on shares traded on stock exchange held for more than 1 year.[^5]                                                                                      |
| **Slovenia**       | 0–15%           | CGT reduces over time: 15% after 10 years, tax-free after 15 years.[^3]                                                                                       |
| **Switzerland**    | 0%              | No CGT on private stock gains for individuals.[^3][^5]                                                                                                        |
| **Turkey**         | 0%              | No CGT for listed shares held over 1 year.[^3][^6]                                                                                                            |
| **South Korea**    | 0%              | No CGT for non-major shareholders in listed stocks.[^3][^7]                                                                                                   |
| **New Zealand**    | 0%              | No general CGT on shares or investments.[^3][^8]                                                                                                              |
| **Mexico**         | 10%             | Flat 10% CGT on stock market gains.[^3][^9]                                                                                                                   |
| **United Kingdom** | 10%             | CGT at 10% within basic-rate band, with annual exemption (£6,000 in 2023–24, expected to rise in future).[Note: Planned rate rise from 2024 onwards][^3][^10] |
| **United States**  | 0–15%           | 0% CGT for incomes up to $44,625 (single filer, 2024). 15% beyond this threshold.[^3][^11]                                                                    |
| **Mexico**         | 10%             | Flat 10% CGT on stock exchange trades.[^3][^8]                                                                                                                |
